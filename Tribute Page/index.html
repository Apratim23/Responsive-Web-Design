Albert Einstein

14 March 1879 - 18 April 1955

Albert Einstein's Notable Works

  • 1905 - Special Theory of Relativity: Revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and motion, introducing concepts like time dilation and length contraction.
  • 1915 - General Theory of Relativity: Extended the principles of relativity to include gravity, explaining it as a curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.
  • 1905 - Photoelectric Effect: Explained how light interacts with matter, leading to the development of quantum mechanics and earning him the Nobel Prize in Physics.
  • 1905 - Brownian Motion: Provided strong evidence for the existence of atoms and molecules, solidifying the atomic theory.
